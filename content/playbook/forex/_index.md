## The Golden Rule

> There are no set rules.
>
> Every market moment is independent and unique. At best, it's an intuitive coin flip with the odds in your favor. At worst, you live and die by these rules — becoming so rigid that you believe they can provide certainty in the most uncertain arena of all.

Use what follows as a framework, not a formula.

---

### Non-Negotiables

**1. A trending DXY is the prerequisite for every trade.**
Avoid 15-minute balance ranges like the plague. A range-bound Dollar makes everything harder than it needs to be — even the best setups cannot contend with a directionless DXY. If the Dollar isn't moving, neither are you.

**2. A currency testing or leaving the extremes of the previous profile is a good start.**
Context matters. Know where price came from before deciding where it's going.

**3. One-time framing is your friend.**
When the market is one-time framing in your direction, let it work. Don't fight the structure.

**4. Let the wave establish itself.**
This is not the NQ or ES. Forex moves at its own pace. Let the nodes form, confirm, then ride them. Rushing the entry is how you get shaken out before the move begins.

**5. If the reference level is still intact, hold the trade.**
Stay in until the next reference area. Premature exits are a tax on your edge — trust the level until the market tells you otherwise.
