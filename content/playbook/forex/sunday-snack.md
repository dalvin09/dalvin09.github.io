## Concept

The Sunday Snack is the rarest setup in the playbook — and one of the highest conviction when it appears. It lives specifically on **USDJPY** and is born from major Japanese news events on Sunday: presidential elections, BOJ decisions, or any red folder catalyst significant enough to move the yen with force.

The volatility this produces is unlike anything seen during the standard trading week. Range expansion is not a possibility here — it is an expectation. The edge is not in predicting the direction before the news, it is in reading the nodes *after* the release and positioning with the move once the market has shown its hand.

> Do not get ahead of the news. Let it drop, let the auction respond, then trade what you see — not what you think will happen.

When this setup is right, it can go **far**. Further than feels reasonable. That is the nature of Sunday news on a thin market. Respect the range, cut size if you must, but do not underestimate what this trade is capable of.

---

## Pre-Event Tells

Before the Sunday event, the yen may give you a warning:

- **Days of overlapping value** on USDJPY in the week prior — the market coiling, building energy before the release
- Reduced range, compressed profiles, value areas that barely move day to day
- This compression is not a signal to trade — it is a signal to *watch*

---

## Criteria

- [ ] **Sunday** session — this setup does not apply during the standard week
- [ ] Major Japanese red folder event on the calendar (election, BOJ, major macro release)
- [ ] USDJPY shows notable volatility reaction immediately following the news
- [ ] Nodes from the news catalyst period are identifiable and clean
- [ ] Context is clear — do not force direction, let the auction reveal it

---

## Entry

- **Trigger:** News drops and creates a clear directional move — wait for the nodes to establish
- **Entry:** Off the HVN or halfback of the news catalyst period, in the direction of the move
- **Stop:** Beyond the node — if the level fails, the read was wrong
- **Size:** **Cut size.** This is a volatile, wide-ranging trade. Smaller position, larger move.
- **Target:** Hold until the **next profile** — this trade has the legs to run well beyond a typical target
- **Exit:** If the position is in good shape after the release, hold. Let the move develop. Trail aggressively but do not exit early out of discomfort.

---

## Rules

1. **Sunday only.** Do not apply this framework to weekday news events — the conditions are fundamentally different.
2. **USDJPY only.** This setup is built around yen-specific volatility in a thin Sunday market.
3. Do not anticipate the direction before the release. Wait for the market to move, then trade the nodes.
4. Cut size on entry. Volatility will test your conviction — smaller size makes it easier to hold through the noise.
5. If the trade is working after the news drops, **hold to the next profile**. Premature exits on this setup leave the majority of the move on the table.
6. Be mindful of context. Not every Sunday news event qualifies — the magnitude of the event matters. A minor release is not a Sunday Snack.

## Notes

The Sunday Snack is rare by nature. The conditions — Sunday session, major Japanese catalyst, clean auction response — do not align often. That rarity is what makes it SSS rank. When it sets up correctly, it is one of the most asymmetric trades in the playbook.

The biggest mistake with this setup is impatience: either entering before the news trying to predict direction, or exiting too early once the move begins. Both mistakes share the same root — distrust of the range. This market can move in ways that feel uncomfortable. That discomfort, when the thesis is intact, is the trade working exactly as it should.

Watch for the yen compression in the days prior. When the week looks quiet and overlapping on USDJPY, check the calendar for Sunday.
