## Concept

**HVN** stands for High Volume Node — a price level where a significant amount of volume was transacted, acting as a magnet and support/resistance zone. The HVN Rush occurs around **red folder economic news events**. The idea is simple: let the news create the momentum, then wait for price to pull back and test the HVN or halfback of the news catalyst period before entering in the direction of the move.

> Patience and precision are non-negotiable with this setup.

---

## Criteria (All must be met)

- [ ] Red folder news event on the calendar
- [ ] The dollar is **not** range bound — must have directional context
- [ ] News drops and creates a clear momentum reaction
- [ ] In the next 30-minute profile, price retraces to test:
  - The **HVN from the news catalyst period**, OR
  - The **halfback of the news catalyst period**
- [ ] Entry level is clean and defined — 5 pip max risk

---

## Entry

- **Trigger:** Price retraces into HVN or halfback of the news catalyst period in the 30-min profile following the news
- **Entry:** At the HVN or halfback level
- **Stop:** Other side of the HVN or halfback level (5 pips max)
- **Target:** High or low of the news catalyst period
- **Exit:** 80% of position at target — leave a runner

---

## Rules

1. Wait for the news. Do not anticipate.
2. Wait for the momentum reaction to complete.
3. Do not chase — only enter on the retrace to HVN or halfback.
4. 5 pips of risk maximum. If the level doesn't give a clean 5-pip stop, skip the trade.
5. Exit 80% at the news period high/low. Trail the runner.

## Notes

The dollar being range bound is a disqualifier — this setup needs fuel. Red folder news provides that fuel. The HVN or halfback acts as the launching pad after the initial reaction. Timing the entry is everything; being early or late by a few pips changes the risk profile entirely.
