## Concept

The Halfback Retest is not a mechanical setup — it is a read. The halfback of the current or prior profile acts as a pivotal balance point. When price returns to test that level *meaningfully* and holds, the market is rejecting the midpoint and signaling a continuation toward the profile's extreme.

What makes this setup nuanced is that the context surrounding each instance will look different. The profile in question, the strength of the test, and the broader market conditions all vary. No two winning trades using this setup will look exactly alike — and that is by design. Recognizing it requires screen time, not a checklist.

> This setup is hard to spot and even harder to trust. That difficulty is part of what gives it edge.

---

## Criteria

- [ ] Identify the halfback of the **current or prior profile**
- [ ] Price returns to test that halfback in a **meaningful way** — not a graze, a genuine test
- [ ] The halfback **holds** — price is rejected at or near the level
- [ ] Context supports continuation: trending DXY, prior profile structure, or session alignment

---

## Entry

- **Trigger:** Meaningful test of the halfback that holds
- **Entry:** At or near the halfback level after the hold is confirmed
- **Stop:** Beyond the halfback — if the level gives way, the thesis is invalid
- **Target:** The **extreme of the profile whose halfback was tested**
- **Exit:** Target the profile extreme; trail a runner if momentum is strong

---

## Rules

1. The test must be **meaningful** — a tick into the level does not qualify. Look for a genuine revisit with a clear reaction.
2. The halfback must **hold**. A close through the level invalidates the setup.
3. Know *which* profile's halfback you are trading. Current profile and prior profile are both valid, but the target changes accordingly.
4. Context matters more here than in any other setup. A range-bound DXY or conflicting structure on the higher timeframe reduces conviction significantly.
5. No two instances of this setup will look alike. Do not force pattern-matching — read the auction.

## Notes

The Halfback Retest earns its Rank B not because the reward is lower, but because the read required to execute it correctly is genuinely difficult. The halfback is always there — the edge is in knowing *when* the test matters and *why* the hold is significant in that specific context.

When it clicks, the trade is clean: defined risk at the halfback, clear target at the profile extreme. When it doesn't click, walk away. Forcing this setup is how you lose on a B-grade trade that should have been a pass.
