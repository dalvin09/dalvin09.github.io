## Concept

The **Overnight Halfback** is the midpoint of the overnight session's range. When the market intraday pulls back to — or rips higher into — this level, it creates one of the highest probability fade opportunities available. The context of how price arrives at this level is everything. This trade is as nuanced as it is profitable, and it is one of the most difficult to execute correctly.

> This trade requires patience, context, and discipline. It is particularly allergic to blind sizing.

---

## Criteria (All must be met)

- [ ] A clear catalyst exists — news event, gap up or gap down, or similar driver
- [ ] Price is moving toward the overnight halfback intraday
- [ ] **A period** tests the overnight halfback (later periods can work but carry lower odds)
- [ ] The overnight halfback holds on the test — no acceptance through it
- [ ] Market context supports a directional move, not a choppy/whipsaw environment
- [ ] No Fed testimony, FOMC, or similar events that create early morning volatility without direction

---

## Entry

- **Trigger:** A period test of the overnight halfback that holds
- **Entry:** Fade the overnight halfback at the test — long if gap down / closing gap, short if gap up / fading gap
- **Stop:** Acceptance through the overnight halfback level
- **Target:** Value area builds breakaway in the direction of the fade — prior day high/low or value area extremes
- **Sizing:** Size is appropriate here — but only when all criteria are confirmed. Never size blindly into this level.

---

## The Setup in Context

**Bullish scenario:** Gap up opens, market begins to close the gap, A period pulls back into the overnight halfback and holds. Odds are strong that value will build and break away higher from this point. The halfback acted as support — the gap is defended and the move resumes.

**Bearish scenario:** Gap down opens, market rips back up into the overnight halfback in A period and stalls. Odds favor the fade lower. The halfback acts as resistance — sellers defend the level and price breaks lower.

---

## Weaknesses & When to Avoid

- **Whipsaw volatility early in the morning** — if the open is chaotic and directional conviction is unclear, stand aside
- **Choppy, low-conviction days** — this trade needs fuel; a dull tape kills it
- **Fed testimony, FOMC days, and similar events** — these create noise before direction; the halfback level becomes unreliable as price whips through it
- **Treating it as a sure thing** — the SSS rank reflects ceiling potential, not guaranteed outcome. Blind sizing into this level is the fastest way to give back gains

## Notes

The formation context is the foundation. A gap that creates the overnight halfback test in A period is the cleanest version of this trade. Later period tests are possible but carry significantly lower odds. The more confluence — catalyst, A period timing, clean hold of the level — the more conviction is warranted in sizing.
