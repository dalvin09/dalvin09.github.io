## Rationale

The setup looked like an overnight halfback trade. Both ES and NQ failed to see follow through initially, and overlapping value had been absent for a while — a sign of directional intent. The thesis was that price would hold below the overnight halfback and build value lower, with yesterday's value area as the next meaningful target.

---

## Execution

Price did move lower initially and tested yesterday's value area, confirming the directional bias. However, as Fed Chair Walsh began testifying, a sharp reversal hit the position. The volatility from the testimony took the trade out before the thesis could fully develop. Position was closed at $0.78.

---

## Mistake / Lesson

First mind was right — after the testimony concluded, the market resumed lower and value did in fact build overlapping, exactly as the thesis called for. The entry cost was already paid to be in the trade. Should have held through the testimony volatility rather than closing into the noise. This is a known weakness of the Overnight Halfback setup on Fed days — the setup itself wasn't wrong, the exit was.
