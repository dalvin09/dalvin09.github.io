Setup: The 45 Degree Break — Rank B

Concept#

The 45 Degree Break is a failure-to-extend trade. The name comes from the visual signature left by aggressive one-time framing across one or two prior profiles — a steep, directional climb that carves out a clean 45-degree angle on the chart. That kind of conviction leaves a mark.

Then the prior profile balances. The market pauses, builds value, and the extension stalls. When the current profile opens and attempts to continue in the direction of that established angle — and fails — the setup is live. The market has rejected the extension. Target the next reference area in the opposite direction.

This applies to any forex pair, but the edge sharpens considerably when the Dollar is adding macro pressure in the same direction as the fade. Sunday sessions are a particularly fertile environment for this setup.

The conviction of the prior move is what makes the failure meaningful. Without the setup, there is no break.

Pre-Trade Signature#

What you are looking for in the profiles leading up to the trade:

  • 1–2 prior profiles: Strong directional conviction, aggressive one-time framing, steep angle of movement — the “45 degree” slope is visible and clear
  • Prior profile: Balance develops — overlapping value, the trend stalls, energy compresses
  • Current profile: Attempts to extend in the direction of the established trend — and fails to do so

That failure is the signal.

Criteria#

  • 1–2 prior profiles show aggressive one-time framing establishing a clear 45-degree directional angle
  • The most recent prior profile balances — value overlaps, extension stalls
  • Current profile opens and makes an attempt to extend in the trend direction
  • That extension fails — price cannot sustain the move and reverses
  • Next reference area below (or above, depending on direction) is identifiable and clean
  • Dollar macro context supports the fade (preferred, not required)

Entry#

  • Trigger: Current profile fails to extend — the attempted continuation breaks down
  • Entry: On confirmation of the failure, near the high/low of the failed extension attempt
  • Stop: Beyond the failed extension — if price reclaims the level, the setup is invalidated
  • Target: Next reference area in the opposite direction
  • Exit: Target the reference area; trail a runner if momentum carries further

Rules#

  1. The prior trending move must be genuine — aggressive OTF, steep angle, clear conviction. A weak drift does not qualify.
  2. Balance in the prior profile is required. Without the pause, there is no coil to break.
  3. Wait for the current profile’s failure to confirm. Do not anticipate the reversal before the extension attempt plays out.
  4. Know your next reference area before entry. That is your target — do not wing it mid-trade.
  5. Dollar macro pressure in the direction of the fade adds conviction. If the Dollar is aligned, trust the setup more. If it is conflicting, reduce size or pass.
  6. Sunday is the preferred session for this setup — thin conditions amplify the failure and the subsequent move.

Notes#

The 45 Degree Break earns its Rank B through nuance and rarity, not lack of quality. The setup requires a specific sequence of profile events — trending conviction, balance, failed extension — that does not appear often. When it does, the read has to be right on all three counts.

The most common mistake is entering too early, before the current profile has confirmed the failure. Watching a strong trend and assuming it will break is not the setup — the setup is the break itself. Let the market attempt the extension, let it fail, then act.

On Sundays with thin conditions and a cooperative Dollar, this trade can move cleanly to the reference target with minimal noise. That combination — Sunday session, macro alignment, confirmed failure — is when this setup is at its best.
